Dablanky 1: Alice Springs

Morning: Fly into Alice Springs, a central hub for exploring the Outback.
Afternoon: Start your Outback adventure by visiting the Alice Springs Desert Park. Learn about the unique flora and fauna of the region, and catch the informative bird of prey and nocturnal animal shows.
Evening: Head to Anzac Hill to witness a stunning sunset over Alice Springs, and enjoy the view of the town and landscape.
Day 2: West MacDonnell Ranges

Morning: Explore Standley Chasm in the morning when the sunlight creates a beautiful play of colors on the rocks.
Afternoon: Visit Simpsons Gap to see the towering red rock formations and then head to the Ochre Pits to discover the importance of ochre to Aboriginal culture.
Evening: Stay at Glen Helen Gorge, where you can enjoy the sunset over the waterhole.

Day 3: Kings Canyon

Morning: Take a luxury tour bus to Kings Canyon, stopping at viewpoints along the way for breathtaking scenery.
Afternoon: Take the Kings Canyon Rim Walk to experience stunning landscapes and potentially spot wildlife. Keep an eye out for the elusive black-footed rock wallaby.
Evening: Consider camping under the stars or staying at the Kings Canyon Resort.

Day 4: Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park

Morning: Bus to Uluru for a memorable sunrise. The changing colors of Uluru are a sight to behold.
Afternoon: Explore Kata Tjuta, a series of large, domed rock formations. Take a walk through the Valley of Wind to explore the unique Outback environment.
Evening: Conclude your adventure with the Sounds of Silence Dinner, where you can enjoy a gourmet meal under the stars.
